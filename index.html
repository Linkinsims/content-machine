MODULE 01

CONTENT STRATEGY
FRAMEWORK

Before you create a single piece of content, you need a strategy. This module shows you exactly how to define your audience, position your product, and set up a content system that works.

STEP 1 — DEFINE YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE

The 3-Question Audience Map

Answer these three questions before anything else. Every piece of content you make will be aimed at this person.

  • Who is buying your product? — Age, gender, lifestyle, income level, values. Be specific. "Women 25–35 who work desk jobs and struggle with back pain" beats "adults."
  • What is their #1 daily frustration? — The problem your product solves. Write it in their own words, not marketing language.
  • What do they want their life to look like? — The result they're after. This is your content's destination.
🎯
AUDIENCE TEMPLATE
My customer is: [specific person]

They struggle with: [specific problem]

They've tried: [failed solutions]

They want: [desired outcome]

They spend time on: [platforms/communities]
💡
RESEARCH SOURCES
Where to find real audience language: — Amazon reviews for similar products
— Reddit threads in your niche
— TikTok comments on competitor videos
— Facebook groups in your niche
— 1-star reviews (what they hate about alternatives)

Copy their exact words. Use them in your scripts.

STEP 2 — PRODUCT POSITIONING

Every product needs one clear positioning statement. This becomes the spine of all your content.

POSITIONING FORMULA Problem → Solution → Result
PROBLEM
"Most [your customer] struggle with [specific problem]. It causes [specific pain] every single day."
SOLUTION
"[Product name] is the [category] that actually [key benefit] — without [the thing they hate about alternatives]."
RESULT
"Now you can [desired outcome] without [sacrifice/effort/cost]."

STEP 3 — THE 4 CONTENT PILLARS

Every piece of content you create belongs to one of these four pillars. Rotate through all four to stay interesting and effective.

01
EDUCATE
Teach your audience something relevant. Build trust. Position yourself as the expert in your niche.
02
ENTERTAIN
Make people stop scrolling. Humor, drama, surprise, or relatability. This is what gets you shared.
03
PROOF
Show results. Reviews, transformations, before/after, UGC. This is what converts viewers into buyers.
04
SELL
Direct product demos, comparisons, features, pricing. Only 1 in 4 posts should be pure selling.

STEP 4 — HOOK STRATEGY

The first 1–3 seconds of a video (or first line of a caption) determines whether anyone watches. A bad hook = no views, regardless of the rest. Here's how to construct one.

THE HOOK FORMULA: Pattern Interrupt + Curiosity Gap

  • Pattern interrupt — Say or show something that breaks the scroll. A bold statement, a weird visual, an unexpected question.
  • Curiosity gap — Tease the payoff without giving it away. "You'll never believe what happened..." or "Most people don't know this..."
  • Personal relevance — Call out your exact audience so they feel it's made for them. "If you own a dog..." / "Every gym owner needs to see this..."
  • Keep it under 3 seconds — Say or show your hook immediately. No intros. No "hey guys." No logo animation.

CONTENT IDEAS
ENGINE

Use these formulas to generate unlimited content ideas for any product in any niche. Each formula is a reusable structure — just swap in your product details.

10 HOOK FORMULAS

H01
The Controversial Statement

Make a bold claim that challenges conventional thinking. Gets people to stop and react.

"Stop using [common product/method]. Here's why it's making the problem worse."
H02
The Direct Call-Out

Speak directly to a specific person. Makes the right viewer feel instantly seen.

"If you [specific problem], this is for you."
H03
The Curiosity Number

Numbers promise a specific, finite payoff. People stay to see all of them.

"3 reasons [your product] is replacing [common alternative]."
H04
The Story Open

Start mid-story at the most interesting moment. Viewer must watch to understand what happened.

"I almost sent this back... until I tried it for one week."
H05
The Warning

Triggers protective instinct. People don't want to miss information that could help them.

"Warning: Do NOT buy [category] until you watch this."
H06
The Mistake Reveal

Everyone relates to making mistakes. Creates instant empathy and curiosity.

"I wasted $[amount] before I found this."
H07
The Before/After Tease

Show or describe the transformation. Viewer watches to see how the change happened.

"This is what [problem area] looked like before. Now look."
H08
The Relatable Struggle

Describe a frustration so precisely that the viewer feels you're reading their mind.

"You know that feeling when [specific annoying problem]? Yeah. This fixes it."
H09
The Secret/Hack Frame

Positions the content as exclusive knowledge. FOMO keeps them watching.

"Nobody talks about this when it comes to [product category]."
H10
The Challenge/Bet

Dares the viewer. Competitive instinct keeps them engaged.

"I bet you've never seen a [product] do THIS."

"I tried [3 alternatives]. None of them worked. Until this."

Expose a problem people don't know they have — then show how your product solves it.

"Most people don't realise their [item] is actually causing [negative outcome]."

10 BEFORE / AFTER IDEAS

VISUAL TRANSFORMATION
Before: messy, broken, ugly, inefficient state. After: clean, fixed, beautiful, optimised state. Show side by side.
ROUTINE TRANSFORMATION
Before: complicated 10-step process. After: simple 2-step process with your product. Time saved = powerful proof.
EMOTIONAL STATE
Before: frustrated, stressed, embarrassed. After: confident, relieved, happy. Show the emotional journey in 30 seconds.
RESULT TIMELINE
Day 1 vs Day 7 vs Day 30. Document a real customer's results over time. Each update is a separate piece of content.
COST COMPARISON
Before: spending $X/month on [alternative]. After: one-time $Y purchase. Make the maths undeniable.
QUALITY COMPARISON
Cheap generic product vs yours. Show the difference in construction, material, or performance directly on camera.
THE MESSY SPACE
Before: cluttered, disorganised. After: clean, functional. Works for home, office, car, kitchen — any organising product.
SKILL LEVEL
Before: needing expertise or experience. After: anyone can do it with your product. Lowers barrier to purchase.
SPEED COMPARISON
Before: [X minutes] doing it the hard way. After: [Y seconds] with your product. Record both. Let the clock speak.
SOCIAL PROOF RESULT
Screenshot/comment before discovering product vs. what they say 30 days later. Real words from real customers.

S09
The Unexpected Use Case

Customer using your product in a way you didn't expect. Expands perceived value and generates curiosity.

S10
The "Worth Every Penny" Story

Addresses price objection directly. Show ROI — emotional, financial, or practical — that makes the cost irrelevant.

MODULE 04

PRODUCT PAGE
CONTENT SYSTEM

Your product page is where traffic turns into money. This module shows you how to build pages that convert — and how to connect your content directly to your store.

THE 6-SECTION PRODUCT PAGE STRUCTURE

1

HEADLINE + SUBHEADLINE

The headline names the result. The subheadline names the problem it solves and who it's for.

  • Headline formula: "[Result] without [sacrifice]" — e.g. "Sleep better tonight without pills or noise."
  • Subheadline formula: "For [specific customer] who [specific problem]."
  • Never lead with the product name. Lead with the outcome.
2

HERO VISUAL / VIDEO

The first image or video on your product page must show the product in use — not just the product in a box. Show the result.

  • Embed your best TikTok/Reels demo video here — it auto-qualifies the buyer
  • Use a real person using the product in a real setting
  • Show the before state or the after result in the first 3 seconds
3

BENEFITS SECTION (NOT FEATURES)

List what the customer gets, not what the product has. Use the "so that" test: Feature + "so that" + Benefit.

  • "Waterproof coating so that you never have to worry about spills ruining it."
  • "Compact design so that it fits anywhere in your bag without adding weight."
  • Aim for 5–7 bullet benefits, ordered by most important first
4

SOCIAL PROOF BLOCK

Insert your best reviews here. Do not bury them at the bottom. Reviews close more sales than any copy you write.

  • Feature 3 reviews minimum — choose ones with specific results, not vague praise
  • Include star rating, reviewer name, and if possible their photo or context ("verified buyer")
  • If you have UGC video reviews, embed them here — they outperform text reviews
5

FAQ (OBJECTION HANDLER)

List the 5 most common reasons someone hesitates to buy — and answer each one directly.

  • "Will this work for my [specific situation]?" — Yes, here's why
  • "What if it doesn't work for me?" — Answer with your refund policy
  • "How long does shipping take?" — Give a specific timeframe
  • "Is this the same as [competitor]?" — Show your differentiation
  • "Is it worth the price?" — Reframe value vs. cost
6

CTA + GUARANTEE

The final section should make it risk-free and urgent to buy.

  • Button text should state the result: "Get Better Sleep Tonight" not just "Add to Cart"
  • Include a guarantee (30-day, 60-day) — reduces risk perception and increases conversion
  • Add a trust statement: "Join [X] customers who already [result]"

MATCHING CONTENT TO PRODUCT PAGES

THE MESSAGE MATCH RULE

  • If your content hook says "Say goodbye to [problem]" — your product page headline must echo that exact language
  • When a viewer lands from a video about [specific benefit], that benefit must be front and centre on the page
  • Use the same words your customers use in reviews. Don't clean them up or make them "professional"
  • If a video is outperforming — embed it directly on the product page. Use proof where it's most valuable
MODULE 05

ORGANIC GROWTH
WORKFLOW

A repeatable system for posting, testing, and doubling down on what drives real results. Remove guesswork. Follow the process.

THE POSTING SCHEDULE

StageDaily PostsPlatformsGoal
Launch (Weeks 1–4)1 per day minimumTikTok + Instagram ReelsBuild baseline data. Find what resonates.
Growth (Months 2–3)1–2 per dayTikTok + Reels + YouTube ShortsIncrease output. Test more formats.
Scale (Month 4+)2–3 per dayAll platforms + PinterestRepurpose winners. Max distribution.

THE MOST IMPORTANT RULE

Consistency beats quality — especially at the start. A good video posted every day beats a perfect video posted once a week. The algorithm rewards posting cadence. Your goal in the first 30 days is to post every single day without exception.

HOW TO TEST CONTENT

1

WEEK 1–2: TEST HOOKS

Make the same video with 3 different hooks. Same body. Same CTA. Only the first 3 seconds change. Whichever hook gets the highest watch time wins.

  • Track: View count, Watch time %, Profile clicks
  • Winner = highest average watch time percentage
2

WEEK 3–4: TEST FORMATS

Take your winning hook and test it in 3 different video formats: talking head, voiceover demo, and UGC-style.

  • Track: Saves, Shares, Link clicks
  • Winner = highest save + share rate (these indicate real interest)
3

MONTH 2: TEST CTAs

Take your winning format and test 3 different CTAs: link in bio, comment keyword, and direct DM prompt.

  • Track: Comments, Profile visits, DMs received, Conversions
  • Winner = most direct path to a sale
4

ONGOING: IDENTIFY WINNERS

A "winner" = any video that significantly outperforms your average. Use this benchmark:

  • More than 3x your average views = winner
  • More than 5% save rate = winning content worth expanding
  • Any video driving store traffic = replicate and iterate immediately
5

DOUBLE DOWN: THE WINNER PROTOCOL

When a video wins, your job for the next 7 days is to make 5 variations of it.

  • Same hook, different product angle
  • Same format, new customer story
  • Same structure, updated visuals or B-roll
  • Remake the exact video with a different person (UGC variation)
  • Turn the audio into a voiceover version
MODULE 06

DAILY CONTENT
SYSTEM

One video per day. 55 minutes total. Here is the exact routine — what to do, when to do it, and how to do it fast.

15
RESEARCH
  • Check TikTok/IG for trending sounds in your niche
  • Scroll competitor comments for new pain points
  • Review your own analytics from yesterday's post
  • Pick ONE content idea from your Ideas Engine
  • Note the hook, angle, and key message you'll use
10
SCRIPT
  • Open the correct template from Module 03
  • Fill in all blanks — do not improvise from scratch
  • Write your hook word-for-word (memorise it)
  • Body: max 3 key points only
  • CTA: choose one, write it out fully
25
RECORD
  • Set up phone vertically (9:16 ratio)
  • Natural light preferred — face or product near window
  • Record hook 3–5 times — use the best take
  • Record body section — short takes are fine, editing is quick
  • Do a quick rough edit in CapCut or native app
  • Add captions (auto-captions are fine)
5
POST
  • Upload to TikTok first (highest organic reach)
  • Write caption: hook line + 3–5 relevant hashtags
  • Cross-post to Instagram Reels immediately after
  • Log the video in your Content Tracker (see below)
  • Check back in 2 hours for early performance signals

YOUR CONTENT TRACKER — WHAT TO LOG EVERY DAY

Create a simple spreadsheet with these columns. Review it every Sunday to find patterns.

  • Date — When you posted
  • Hook Used — Which hook formula (H01–H10)
  • Format — Talking head, voiceover, UGC, demo, etc.
  • Content Pillar — Educate, Entertain, Proof, or Sell
  • Views — At 24h and 7 days
  • Watch Time % — The algorithm's most important metric
  • Saves + Shares — High intent signals
  • Link Clicks / Sales — Direct revenue attribution if trackable
  • Notes — What worked, what felt off, ideas for follow-up

FILMING SETUP (ZERO BUDGET)

📱
EQUIPMENT
You need nothing except your phone. — Use your existing phone camera
— Natural window light (face window when filming yourself)
— Stack books to prop your phone if needed
— Clean background: plain wall, shelf, or outdoors
— Record audio in a quiet room — turn off fans/AC

The top-performing content on TikTok is regularly filmed on phone cameras in basic settings. What matters is the hook and the message.
✂️
EDITING (5 MINUTES)
CapCut (free) is all you need. — Cut dead air at start and end
— Cut between takes — jump cuts are fine and expected
— Add auto-captions (increases watch time 40%)
— Add one trending sound at low volume if relevant
— Export at 1080p

Do not over-edit. Overly polished content often underperforms raw, authentic content on TikTok.

BUILDING YOUR CONTENT LIBRARY

🏗️
EVERGREEN CONTENT BANK
Build a folder of "evergreen" videos — content that will still be relevant 12 months from now. Problem/solution videos, how-to demos, buying guides, and comparison videos. These can be reposted, boosted, or recycled every 60–90 days.
🔥
WINNER ARCHIVE
Every video that hits 3x your average views gets saved into a "Winner" folder. Study these monthly. What's the common hook? Common format? Common length? Winners tell you exactly what your audience wants. Let them guide your strategy.
📦
UGC COLLECTION SYSTEM
Set up an automated post-purchase email asking happy customers to send a video. Offer a small incentive (discount, gift). Every piece of UGC you collect is free content. 10 UGC videos = 10 additional pieces of highly authentic content that outperforms branded video.

INCREASING OUTPUT WITHOUT BURNOUT

1

BATCH FILMING (WEEKLY)

Set aside 2–3 hours one day per week to film 7–14 videos at once. This is far more efficient than daily filming. Prepare all scripts in advance, wear the same outfit, and film in the same location for consistency.

  • Film all similar formats together (all talking heads, then all demos)
  • Keep a filming checklist so you never miss a step
  • Edit later — never try to film and edit in one session
2

DELEGATION TRIGGER POINTS

When you hit consistent revenue from content, these are the exact tasks to delegate first:

  • First hire: Video editor — handles all CapCut editing and posting
  • Second hire: UGC creator — someone who films content in the style of real customers
  • Third hire: Content scheduler — repurposing, scheduling, cross-posting
  • You keep: strategy, hooks, and identifying winners
3

THE 70/20/10 CONTENT MIX

Once you have winners, follow this content mix to maintain performance:

  • 70% — Proven formats. Variations of what already works.
  • 20% — Experiments. New hooks, angles, or formats you're testing.
  • 10% — Wildcards. Trending audio, viral formats, cultural moments.
4

MONTHLY CONTENT AUDIT

Every 30 days, spend 30 minutes reviewing your Content Tracker. Answer these four questions:

  • What content drove the most profile visits? → Do more of this.
  • What content drove the most link clicks? → This is your sales content. Prioritise it.
  • What content got the most saves? → This is your education content. Build on it.
  • What flopped consistently? → Drop that angle or format entirely.

THE COMPOUND EFFECT OF ORGANIC CONTENT

  • Month 1: You're planting seeds. Most content will underperform. This is normal and necessary.
  • Month 2: You'll notice patterns. 1–2 videos will start to pop. Double down immediately.
  • Month 3: Consistent posting + doubling down on winners = your first traffic spike from organic
  • Month 4–6: Old content keeps generating views. You're building a compounding content asset, not a campaign.
  • Month 6+: A library of 150+ videos means daily organic traffic with no ongoing ad spend. This is the machine.
